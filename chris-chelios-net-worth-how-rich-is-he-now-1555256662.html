Who is Chris Chelios?

Christos Kostas Chelios, born on January 25, 1962, is a former professional ice hockey defenseman hailing from the United States. With a remarkable career in the National Hockey League (NHL), Chelios stands as one of the longest-tenured players in the league's history. 

Article continues below advertisementArticle continues below advertisement

His legacy includes three Stanley Cup championships—one with the Montreal Canadiens and two with the Detroit Red Wings. Chelios showcased his skills for various teams, including the Canadiens, Blackhawks, Red Wings, and the Atlanta Thrashers. 

Notably, during the 2009–10 NHL season, when he was called up from the AHL's Chicago Wolves to join the Thrashers, Chelios held the title of the oldest active player in the NHL and the second oldest in NHL history. 

At that point, he had played the most games of any active NHL player and was the last remaining player from the 1981 NHL Entry Draft still active, spanning drafts up to 1986. Additionally, he boasted the highest career penalty minutes among active players.
