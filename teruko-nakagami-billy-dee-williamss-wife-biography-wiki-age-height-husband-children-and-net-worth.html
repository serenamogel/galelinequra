Teruko Nakagami Biography

Teruko Nakagami is the third wife of an American actor, voice actor, and artist Billy Dee Williams. She was a Japanese American who was born in Chicago. Nakagami was originally named Irene but changed her name to Teruko after meeting Shorter. When Nakagami was six, she and her twin sister were shipped off to a Japanese-American internment camp in California. Billy Dee is best known as Lando Calrissian in the Star Wars franchise, first in the early 1980s, as well as nearly forty years later in The Rise of Skywalker (2019), marking one of the longest intervals between onscreen portrayals of a character by the same actor in American film history.

Billy Dee has been married three times and has three children and two grandchildren. His first marriage was to Audrey Sellers in the year 1959. They were divorced some years later, after which he apparently became depressed. Billy Dee stated that “there was a period when I was very despondent, broke, depressed, my first marriage was on the rocks.”

They had a son, Corey Dee Williams, born in the year 1960. In 1968, Billy Dee married model and actress Marlene Clark in Hawaii. They divorced in the year 1971. Billy Dee moved from New York City to California in 1971.

Billy Dee married Teruko Nakagami on December 27, 1972. Nakagami brought a daughter, Miyako (born 1962), from her previous marriage to musician Wayne Shorter. Together they have a daughter, Hanako (born 1973). In the year 1984, he bought a “Zen-like contemporary” home in the Trousdale Estates neighborhood of Beverly Hills, California; he sold it in the year 2012. Billy Dee filed for an amicable divorce from Nakagami in 1993, but they reconciled and were again living together by 1997.
