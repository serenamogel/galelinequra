Sometimes people get fame mostly because of their better half. Such personality is Tiffany Ortiz, who received all the recognition after getting married to her husband, David Ortiz. In addition to that, she also has a golden heart, which can be seen in her humanitarian work.

The American national Tiffany was born on October 20, 1974. Not just currently, she always has a sports background in her family. Her father was a big fan of the Green Bay Packers, and because of that, she got into sports as well. 

Her Married Life with Her Husband 

In 2002, Tiffany Ortiz and David Ortiz walked down the aisle in a private ceremony. At the time, David was playing an MLB player when the pair met back at a bar. David and Tiffany tied the knot eight years later in 2010. 

Ever since the couple tied a knot, they were never part of any rumors and controversies until 2015. The couple failed to pursue their relationship after 13 years of marriage and chose to file a divorce, but, they reconciled soon after.

The family of five is currently living a conjugal lifestyle. As of now, there are no rumors about their other affairs. 

They Share Three Children

Tiffany shares three beautiful kids with Ortiz. The family of five settled in Weston, Massachusetts, where Ortiz was the Boston Red Sox star player.

Their first daughter Jessica Ortiz was born on 23 October 1996. After four years, Tiffany gave birth to their second daughter, Alexandra Ortiz, who was born on 22 March 2001. The couple added one more child D'Angelo Ortiz to their family on July 10, 2004.

There is no doubt that as a humanitarian and businessman, Tiffany earns a decent amount of cash from her job.  Although she has not spoken publicly about her net worth, her wealth is estimated at $10 million according to online sources.

On the other hand, her husband David Ortiz has a fortune of $45 million. While playing as a baseball player, he gained $160 million. His maximum wage was $16 million, which he earned in both his final two seasons in 2015 and 2016.

Moreover, his net worth is further assisted by endorsements with businesses such as MasterCard, JetBlue, Coca-Cola, and Wild Wings Buffalo. Both former husband and wife are engaged in the work of the charity.

In 2007, the duo established The David Ortiz Children's Fund. He also released his own Charity Wine label in 2008, with all the proceeds going to David Ortiz Children's Fund. The Vintage Papi wine raised $150,000 for charity.
